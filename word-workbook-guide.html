Mastering Tricky Vowel Sounds and Suffixes

When you encounter unfamiliar vowel sounds, vowel combinations, or tricky suffixes: Use the Vowel Combination Chart or the Common Suffixes Chart. These charts solve phonetic problems that continuously pop up in reading.

Example: Struggling with "flew"

Problem: You're making mistakes with "ew", "oi", or "ight" sounds and not making progress.
Solution: Click the Vowel Combination Chart, find "ew" and practice with these words: few, stew, threw, new, chew, and dew. Each word provides audio when clicked.
Power of Repetition: Practice this pattern several times until the "ew" sound becomes automatic.

Example: Struggling with "tion" Suffix

Problem: Difficulty with the "tion" ending
Solution: Click the Common Suffixes button and practice with: intention, exertion, execution, station, potion, and emotion. This targeted repetition builds mastery quickly.
