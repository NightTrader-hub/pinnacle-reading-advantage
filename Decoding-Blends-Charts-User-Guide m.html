üìö Getting Started - Foundation Skills

For best success with the Decoding Blends Charts, students should know:

  • All letter sounds (clean, pure sounds without "uh")
  • Long vowel sounds (vowels saying their names)
  • Short vowel sounds (quick, clipped sounds)

Helpful tip: Students learn best when they know the essence of sounds. The letter 't' makes just the 't' sound, not "tuh". The letter 's' makes just the 's' sound, not "suh". Using pure sounds makes blending much easier and more natural.

There are numerous YouTube videos that help students learn their letter sounds and long and short vowel sounds. It is also very helpful to purchase letter flashcards and practice them a few times a day.

Timer Adjustment Guidelines

  • Student faster than audio: Reduce timer to 3 seconds
  • Student slower than 4 seconds: Increase timer to 5+ seconds
  • Key principle: Set the time to just above student's current response time
  • Progressive goal: Gradually work down to 1 second

Special Rules for Consonant Rows

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Start with C and G Rows

Why these first? Students must learn the special sound changes:

  • C + e or i = S sound
    Examples: city, civil, circus, cell, cent, cement
  • G + e or i = J sound
    Examples: gentle, page, George, giant, agile, magic

If students don't master these rules early, they'll continue making mistakes throughout their learning.
